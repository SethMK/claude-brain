# Wanderloots

## Role

YouTube creator (76.9K subscribers as of April 2026) covering **Obsidian, personal knowledge management (PKM), digital gardening, and Zettelkasten**. Real first name **Callum**. Former intellectual property lawyer / patent agent — quit 3 years ago to focus on PKM and AI integration [[2026-04-25-wanderloots-why-llm-wiki]]. Has run a personal Obsidian vault for the entire 3-year span; the graph view in his recent video shows thousands of nodes accumulated one note at a time.

The IP-lawyer background shapes his framing: he reaches for *invention* and *book* examples (flywheel, "The One Thing") rather than software analogies — useful for the non-dev audience this project targets.

## Why I track them

- **Non-dev framing.** Most LLM-Wiki content is written by developers using Claude Code. Wanderloots speaks to PKM/researcher audiences — closer to Marcin's lens.
- **Long Obsidian runway.** Their vault predates the Karpathy gist by years, so they can speak to *what compounds in a knowledge graph over 3+ years* — not just "I set this up yesterday."
- **Theory-side coverage.** While the YouTube ecosystem has dozens of "set up Karpathy's wiki in 12 minutes" tutorials, Wanderloots covers the **why**: graphs vs RAG, scaling effects, future of human-AI shared memory.

## Key claims / patterns

- *"A shared memory layer for EVERY AI tool"* — the core pitch: build the wiki in tool-agnostic markdown so it survives any LLM you switch to [[2026-04-25-wanderloots-why-llm-wiki]].
- *"The knowledge isn't lost, it's just trapped in the silos of each tool."* — the cross-tool memory framing.
- **Graph RAG > standard RAG** for complex datasets. (See [[Knowledge graph]].)
- **Three elements** of a knowledge graph: **node, edge, triple** (subject-relationship-object). The graph itself is *emergent* from disciplined linking, not designed.
- **Two-vault pattern** — keeps a *human vault* (his own thinking) separate from an *LLM vault / LLM Wiki* (AI-generated synthesis), so it's clear what came from him vs the model. This project takes the opposite tack (single vault, all LLM-maintained); his pattern is a known variant worth tracking.
- The LLM Wiki concept *predates* Karpathy's gist — knowledge graphs in PKM communities are a multi-year practice. The gist made it viral, not novel.
